The secret to the perfect "Kevin Dorée" crunch without burning the butter?

After weeks of testing in my home kitchen, I've finally discovered that the traditional French technique of "beurre noisette" is only half the battle. To achieve that legendary golden snap we all crave, the moisture content must be strictly controlled.

I start by infusing the butter with a touch of Madagascar vanilla, but here is the trick: I use a slow-convection method at 145°C. This allows the sugars in the grains to caramelize without the butter fats reaching their smoke point. Who else has experimented with this low-and-slow approach?
